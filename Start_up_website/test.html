The Challenge & Our Solution

The Problem

Clinicians need affordable, bedside, long-duration brain monitoring to guide care and track neurocognitive disease.

Unfortunately, current methods are a compromise. Continuous monitoring is often low-specificity (like legacy fNIRS), while high-resolution imaging is intermittent and immobile (like CT/MRI) or highly invasive and complex (like EEG-fMRI or implants).

The Solution: MindHalo™

NeuroTrace's product, the MindHalo™, is a mobile, high-resolution brain-imaging cap.

It combines advanced VCSEL source arrays and SPAD detectors with time-domain gating and coherent MIMO detection. This allows us to create sharper cortical maps and provide absolute hemoglobin quantitation—not just relative trends—right at the bedside or at home.

Our Advantage

Mobility & Cost

The wearable cap and belt controller are designed for flexible use on the ward or even at home.

Resolution & Depth

Our time-gated, coherent detection provides sharper cortical sensitivity than traditional continuous-wave fNIRS.

True Quantification | Data & AI

We deliver absolute estimates for HbO, HbR, and HbT, moving beyond simple relative trends. Our high-SNR, time-resolved datasets create a "data flywheel" to power ML-driven biomarkers and decision support.

Our 24-Month Roadmap

0-6 Months

Execute license, build EVT1 prototypes, complete phantom validation, and establish RUO agreements.

6-12 Months

Begin first-in-human RUO studies across ICU/OR/EMU (n≈40-60) and lock v1 reconstruction algorithms.

12-18 Months

Complete DVT build, perform V&V, and hold FDA Pre-Submission meeting with a clear pivotal plan.

18-24 Months

Submit 510(k) to the FDA, onboard pilot commercial sites, and begin to scale manufacturing.
